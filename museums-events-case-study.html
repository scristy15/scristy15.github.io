UX / UI Case Study

Museums & Events App

A mobile app designed to help users discover museums, art exhibitions, and cultural events in their city. The app focuses on improving event visibility and helping users stay informed through personalized notifications and a simple ticket-purchasing experience.

The Problem

Art students and people interested in cultural events often miss exhibitions, workshops, and museum activities because event communication is limited and scattered across different platforms. Many museums also lack accessible and user-friendly digital experiences, making it difficult for users to quickly discover relevant events or receive reminders.

The Goal

Design a mobile app that allows users to:

  • Discover museums and art events easily
  • Receive personalized notifications about upcoming events
  • Purchase tickets in a simple and accessible way
  • Improve access to cultural activities for users with different accessibility needs

    UX Process

    User research: Summary
    I conducted interviews with art students and people interested in museums and cultural events to better understand their behaviors, frustrations, and needs.
    Key Findings

  • Users often discover events too late
  • Event information is usually fragmented across social media and websites
  • Free events are especially difficult to track
  • Accessibility considerations are frequently overlooked in cultural apps
    • One important insight from the research was the need to design for users with visual impairments. This changed some of my initial assumptions and helped me understand the importance of accessibility in digital products.


    Ana is an Art History student who needs an easy way to discover museum events and receive reminders because she wants to attend more cultural activities without missing important exhibitions, especially free events.

    Paper wireframes

    I started by sketching multiple layout ideas for the ticket purchase and payment flows. This helped me quickly explore navigation patterns and prioritize the most intuitive user experience before moving into digital design.

    Digital wireframes

    After validating the initial concepts, I created digital wireframes in Figma to define the app structure, user flow, and content hierarchy. The design focused on:

  • Simple navigation
  • Mobile-first usability
  • Clear event information
  • Easy ticket purchasing flow

    • Low-Fidelity Prototype

    I created a low-fidelity prototype to test the core user journey, including:

  • Browsing events
  • Viewing event details
  • Purchasing tickets
  • Receiving notifications
    • The prototype allowed early validation of the navigation flow and usability before moving into higher-fidelity designs.
